Historical Timeline of Logical Development from "Epistemology of Logic"
This timeline presents the historical development of logical thought as outlined by Epameinondas Xenopoulos in his work, compiled directly from the book's chronological references.

Pre-Aristotelian Foundations (6th-4th Century BCE)
Pre-Socratic Period

Heraclitus of Ephesus: Dynamic-dialectical principle "everything flows" (τὰ πάντα ῥεῖ); foundation of dialectical thought with contradiction as central principle (pp. 10, 15, 20, 48, 114-116, 274)

Parmenides of Elea: Static principle of identity (Α = Α); origin of formal-static logical thought (pp. 10, 15, 33, 48)

Zeno of Elea: Eleatic dialectics and paradoxes (pp. 105-114)

Eleatic School: Development of dialectical method (pp. 105-114)

Classical Greek Philosophy

Socrates: Method of inductive arguments and seeking universal definitions (p. 122)

Plato: Dialectical method (διαλεκτική), theory of Ideas (εἶδος/ἰδέα), and role in historical development of logical thought (pp. 20, 118-119, 122)

Sophists: Development of dialectical techniques (pp. 116-117)

Aristotelian Foundation (4th Century BCE)
Aristotle (384-322 BCE)

Foundation of formal (traditional) logic with the "Organon"

Development of syllogistic theory, principles of identity, non-contradiction, excluded middle

Taxonomic organization of thought (pp. 20-21, 122-140, 271)

Considered by Kant as the point beyond which logic had not advanced (p. 21)

Post-Aristotelian Development (3rd Century BCE - 17th Century CE)
Hellenistic Period

Stoic School: Development of propositional logic (pp. 140-142)

Medieval Period

Scholastic Logic: Continuation and development of Aristotelian logic through 17th century (pp. 142-147)

Early Modern Period

Francis Bacon (1561-1626): "Novum Organum" and logic as art of discovery (pp. 21, 39)

René Descartes (1596-1650): Contribution to methodology and logical development (p. 21)

Baruch Spinoza (1632-1677): Including dictum "omnis determinatio est negatio" (pp. 21, 54)

Enlightenment & German Idealism (17th-19th Centuries)
Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz (1646-1716)

Project for universal formal language ("Characteristica Universalis") and "Ars Combinatoria"

Precursor to mathematical logic with concept of logical calculus (pp. 21-22, 30-31, 147-151)

Immanuel Kant (1724-1804)

Views on logic, categories, and statement that logic had not advanced since Aristotle (pp. 21, 152-153)

Georg Wilhelm Friedrich Hegel (1770-1831)

Systematic development of idealist dialectical logic

Concepts of becoming, the universal (U), and positive role of contradiction (pp. 10, 22, 24, 33-34, 39, 172-177, 210-212, 274-276)

19th Century Materialist Dialectics

Karl Marx (1818-1883): Materialist inversion of Hegelian dialectics, historical-genetic method, mathematical manuscripts (MMM) (pp. 22, 26, 30-31, 39, 212-219, 276-277)

Friedrich Engels (1820-1895): Co-development of dialectical materialism; "Anti-Dühring" and "Dialectics of Nature" (pp. 22, 27-28, 31, 38)

Mathematical Logic Development (Mid-19th - Early 20th Century)
Foundational Period

George Boole (1815-1864): Founder of mathematical (symbolic) logic; Boolean algebra (pp. 24, 153-155)

Augustus De Morgan (1806-1871): Development of mathematical logic (p. 24)

Late 19th - Early 20th Century

Gottlob Frege (1848-1925): Major contributor to foundations of mathematical logic and formal language (pp. 24, 30, 41, 155-160)

Giuseppe Peano (1858-1932): Contributions to foundations of mathematics and symbolic logic (pp. 24, 166-167)

David Hilbert (1862-1943): Formalist program and axiomatic method in foundations of mathematics (pp. 24, 160-166)

Early 20th Century Logic

Bertrand Russell (1872-1970): "Principia Mathematica" (with Whitehead) and logical atomism (pp. 24, 167-168)

Alfred North Whitehead (1861-1947): Co-author of "Principia Mathematica" (p. 24)

Clarence Irving Lewis (1883-1964): Development of modal logic (pp. 18, 35)

Jan Łukasiewicz (1878-1956): Development of many-valued and three-valued logics (pp. 18, 25, 35)

20th Century Developments & Dialectical Logic
Vladimir Lenin (1870-1924)

Contribution to materialist dialectics; "Philosophical Notebooks" insights on dialectics (pp. 22-23, 29-30, 33-34, 39, 43, 45)

Logical Schools & Movements

Vienna Circle / School of Vienna: Logical positivism (p. 19)

Warsaw School: Development of modern logic (p. 19)

Mid-20th Century Logic

Arend Heyting (1898-1980): Intuitionistic logic (p. 35)

Kurt Gödel (1906-1978): Limitations in formal systems (incompleteness theorems) (pp. 28, 35)

Alonzo Church (1903-1995): Syntactic limitations in formal systems (p. 28)

Alfred Tarski (1901-1983): Semantic theory and concept of truth (pp. 23, 28)

Stephen C. Kleene (1909-1994): Metamathematics (p. 35)

Evert Willem Beth (1908-1964): Foundations of mathematics (p. 35)

Haskell B. Curry (1900-1982): Combinatory logic without variables (p. 35)

Robert Feys (1889-1961): Combinatory logic without variables (p. 35)

Gerhard Gentzen (1909-1945): Proof theory (p. 35)

Frederic B. Fitch (1908-1987): Modal logic (p. 35)

Dialectical Logic Formalization Attempts

Roman Suszko (1919-1979): Non-Fregean logic and formal approaches to dialectical contradiction (pp. 18, 25, 35, 45)

L. Rogowski: Directional logic and formalizing Hegelian theses on contradiction (pp. 18, 25, 45)

S. Jaskowski: Propositional calculus for contradictory systems (pp. 25, 45)

V. Filkorn (or Filcorn): Dynamic-dialectical logic (pp. 25, 35, 44)

G. Gunther: Formalization of dialectical thought structures (pp. 18, 25, 35)

V. Grieniewski (or Greniewski): Extension of formal logic (pp. 25-26, 44)

D. Dubarle: Interpretations and formalizations related to dialectical logic (pp. 25, 35, 49)

Korok: Dynamic-dialectical logic (p. 35)

Genetic Epistemology & Contemporary Synthesis
Jean Piaget (1896-1980)

Genetic epistemology and operational structures of thought (INRC group)

Research extended by Xenopoulos for dialectical solutions in logic (pp. 12-13, 23, 25-26, 44, 48)

Late 20th Century

Arthur N. Prior (1914-1969): Analysis showing modal systems reduce to two-valued logic; "Time and Modality" (pp. 18, 25, 35, 45)

Willard Van Orman Quine (1908-2000): Discussions on truth values and ontology (pp. 13, 24-25, 35)

Soviet & Eastern European Contributions

P. Kopnin (or Kopnine): Views on formal-dialectical logic relationship (pp. 33, 44)

V. Stoliarov: Complementary nature of modern formal and dialectical logic (p. 33)

P. Novikov: "Introduction à la logique mathématique" (p. 26)

Yu. Manin: "A course in Mathematical logic" (p. 26)

Epameinondas Xenopoulos (1923-1994)

Author of "Epistemology of Logic: Logic-Dialectic or Theory of Knowledge"

Synthesis of 2,500 years of Greek philosophical thought into modern mathematical frameworks

Development of formal-dialectical logic integrating static and dynamic dimensions

Work completed posthumously by family (first edition 1998, second edition 2024)

Note: All entries, dates, and page references in this timeline are derived solely from Epameinondas Xenopoulos's "Epistemology of Logic: Logic-Dialectic or Theory of Knowledge" (2nd ed., 2024), its footnotes, and its internal bibliography. No external historical sources, interpretations, or chronological expansions have been made. The timeline reflects the specific historical narrative and periodization as presented in the original text, focusing exclusively on figures and developments explicitly mentioned by Xenopoulos.

Ιστορικό Χρονολόγιο της Λογικής Ανάπτυξης από την «Επιστημολογία της Λογικής»
Αυτό το χρονολόγιο παρουσιάζει την ιστορική ανάπτυξη της λογικής σκέψης όπως περιγράφεται από τον Επαμεινώνδα Ξενόπουλο στο έργο του, συγκεντρωμένο απευθείας από τις χρονολογικές αναφορές του βιβλίου.

Παρμενίδης ο Ελεάτης: Στατική αρχή της ταυτότητας (Α = Α)· απαρχή της τυπικο-στατικής λογικής σκέψης (σσ. 10, 15, 33, 48)

Ζήνων ο Ελεάτης: Ελεατική διαλεκτική και παράδοξα (σσ. 105-114)

Ελεατική Σχολή: Ανάπτυξη της διαλεκτικής μεθόδου (σσ. 105-114)

Κλασική Ελληνική Φιλοσοφία

Σωκράτης: Μέθοδος επαγωγικών επιχειρημάτων και αναζήτηση γενικών ορισμών (σ. 122)

Πλάτων: Διαλεκτική μέθοδος (διαλεκτική), θεωρία των Ιδεών (εἶδος/ἰδέα), και ρόλος στην ιστορική ανάπτυξη της λογικής σκέψης (σσ. 20, 118-119, 122)

Σοφιστές: Ανάπτυξη διαλεκτικών τεχνικών (σσ. 116-117)

Αριστοτελικό Θεμέλιο (4ος αιώνας π.Χ.)
Αριστοτέλης (384-322 π.Χ.)

Θεμελίωση της τυπικής (παραδοσιακής) λογικής με το «Όργανον»

Ανάπτυξη της συλλογιστικής θεωρίας, αρχών ταυτότητας, μη-αντίφασης, αποκλειόμενου τρίτου

Ταξινομική οργάνωση της σκέψης (σσ. 20-21, 122-140, 271)

Θεωρήθηκε από τον Καντ ως το σημείο πέρα από το οποίο η λογική δεν είχε προχωρήσει (σ. 21)
